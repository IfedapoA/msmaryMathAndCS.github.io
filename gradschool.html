Information about graduate school opportunities for Business, Math and CS Department graduates

About Graduate Schools

Many students from Mount St. Mary's University choose to further their education by pursuing graduate degrees at various institutions. The Math and CS Department actively supports students in their graduate school journey through personalized mentoring, application guidance, and connecting current students with alumni who have pursued advanced degrees.

Graduate Schools & Programs

Here are some common graduate schools and programs our students pursue after graduation:

Boston University - Boston, MA

Master's in Computer Science program offers a comprehensive curriculum focusing on theoretical foundations and practical applications.

Core Program Requirements:
  • Theory Course: GRS CS 630 – Theory of Computation
  • Software Course: GRS CS 611 – Software Engineering
  • Systems Course: Choose from:
    • CAS CS 551 – Computer Systems
    • CAS CS 552 – Operating Systems
    • CAS CS 561 – Computer Networks
    • GRS CS 651 – Computer Architecture
    • GRS CS 654 – Distributed Systems
    • GRS CS 655 – Advanced Computer Networks
Student Pathway:

Mount students who attend Boston University often build on a strong foundation in algorithms, discrete math, and software development. The transition to BU’s graduate-level rigor is supported by faculty mentorship and project-based learning. BU’s program also emphasizes research — especially in areas like artificial intelligence, security, and data systems — providing a great opportunity for students interested in academic or industry research roles.

Many students take advantage of BU's location in the Boston tech corridor by pursuing internships or co-op experiences with local startups, research labs, or companies like HubSpot, IBM, and Amazon Robotics.

Mount St. Mary's University - Emmitsburg, MD

MBA program with various focus areas and concentrations. The program offers a flexible format suitable for both traditional students and student-athletes.

Core Curriculum (30 credits):

Students must complete 10 required core courses, including:

  • MBA 501 – Management Skills Development
  • MBA 503 – Financial Reporting and Control
  • MBA 504 – Global Business and Economics
  • MBA 505 – Strategic Marketing
  • MBA 506 – Quantitative & Statistical Methods
  • MBA 509 – Financial Analysis
  • MBA 516 – Organizational Theory & Management Practice
  • MBA 518 – Management & Information Technology
  • MBA 519 – Strategic Analysis & Integrated Decision Making
  • MBA 536 – Business Ethics & Social Responsibility

Note: MBA 501 is recommended early in the program. MBA 519 requires MBA 503, 504, and 509, along with 24 credit hours. MBA 536 requires 24 graduate hours and all prerequisites.

Electives (6 credits):

Students choose two additional electives. Options include general MBA electives or data science-focused courses such as:

  • MDATA 500 – Introduction to Data Science & the Data
  • MDATA 510 – Data Engineering and Data Munging
  • MDATA 520 – Predictive Modeling Algorithms
  • MDATA 530 – Data Science Applications
  • MDATA 600 – Data Science Capstone
Optional MBA Concentrations (12 credits):

Students may specialize in one of the following areas:

  • Economics
  • Finance
  • Healthcare Management
  • International Business
  • Management
  • Marketing
  • Not-for-Profit Management
  • Personnel & Industrial Relations

Each concentration includes:

  • 2 electives in the chosen area
  • 1 related core course
  • MBA 519 with a specialized assignment in the concentration

A formal declaration of concentration is required by the time 18 credits are completed.

Student-Athlete Pathway:

Many student-athletes who retain their NCAA eligibility for a fourth year choose to begin or complete their MBA during this time. The program's flexible online format makes it especially accommodating for those balancing academics and athletics.

Hood College - Frederick, MD

MBA program with various focus areas, including Data Analytics. Program requires 36 credits total.

Program Structure:
  • Foundation Courses (18 credits):
    • ECON 551 - Foundations of Economics
    • MGMT 550 - Business Analytics
    • MGMT 551 - Management Theory
    • MGMT 553 - Foundations of Accounting
    • MGMT 554 - Legal Environment of Business
  • Core Requirements (30 credits):
    • ECON 560 - Managerial Economics
    • MGMT 560 - Leadership & Organizational Behavior
    • MGMT 561 - Financial Management
    • MGMT 562 - Financial & Managerial Accounting
    • MGMT 563 - Marketing Management
    • MGMT 564 - Production & Operations Management
    • MGMT 565 - International Management
    • MGMT 566 - Information Management & Technology
    • MGMT 567 - Organizational Sustainability
    • MGMT 590 - Strategy & Competitive Advantage
  • Data Analytics Focus Area (6 credits):
    • ITMG 516 - Introduction to Data Analytics and Business Data Mining
    • CSIT 512 - Elements of Computer Programming or ITMG 524 - Advanced Data Analytics

These programs offer excellent opportunities for advanced study in technology, business, and analytics fields. Many programs offer both full-time and part-time options to accommodate different student needs.

Preparing for Graduate School

The Math and CS Department offers several resources to help students prepare for graduate studies:

  • Faculty mentorship for selecting programs and specializations
  • GRE preparation guidance
  • Research opportunities to strengthen applications
  • Alumni networking events with current graduate students
  • Application workshops and personal statement reviews

Why Pursue Graduate Studies?

Graduate education provides advanced knowledge and skills, opening doors to higher-level career opportunities, research, and leadership roles in various industries. Benefits include:

  • Higher earning potential and career advancement
  • Specialized expertise in emerging technologies
  • Research and innovation opportunities
  • Professional network expansion
  • Teaching and academic career paths
