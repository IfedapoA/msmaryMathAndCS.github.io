Double Majors in the Math & CS Department

Expand your academic horizons by combining two complementary disciplines

About Double Majors

Double majors allow students to study in two distinct fields that overlap for a more profound understanding of topics. These dual majors span across all of the Math and Science fields. Due to the connectedness of Mount's academic programs, students can complete these dual majors in the same timeframe as a regular degree.

Available Combinations

The following majors can be combined:

  • Mathematics
  • Cybersecurity
  • Data Science
  • Computer Science

Source: MSMU Course Catalog

Program Oversight

The double major programs are overseen by:

  • Dean of the School: Dr. Christine McCauslin (oversees the School of Science, Mathematics and Technology)
  • Department Chair: Dr. Joenelle Hook (oversees Mathematics and Computer Science programs)

Learn more about the School of Science, Mathematics & Technology

Benefits of Double Majoring

  • Complete 2-3 degrees in the same timeframe as a single degree
  • Develop enhanced problem-solving skills across multiple disciplines
  • Access to a vibrant academic community with diverse perspectives
  • More tailored career paths with broader job opportunities
  • Efficient credit overlap reduces overall workload

How to Join

Students can pursue a double major by:

  1. Enrolling as a double major when first declaring
  2. Adding a second major after consulting with your academic advisor

Most Common Double Majors

Although there are more throughout the school, the most common double majors include:

  1. Computer Science + CyberSecurity
  2. Mathematics + Data Science
  3. Mathematics + CyberSecurity
  4. Data Science + CyberSecurity

Since these areas coincide in many professions, learning any combination at The Mount is very beneficial for your professional career

Related Activities & Events

SmallTalks

30-minute to 1-hour presentations by students on research topics ranging from tarot card probability to HTML programming. Open to all campus members.

Mount Programming Club

A student club that meets to solve coding challenges, work on projects, and engage in friendly competitions.

SPARC

University-wide showcase where seniors present their year-long capstone projects to faculty and peers.

Department Clubs

Various student organizations that provide support and networking opportunities for STEM majors.

Check out clubs and events at the Mount P.E.A.K. Page

Important Considerations

  • While efficient, double majors still require careful planning and time management
  • Participating in clubs and study groups enhances the academic experience
  • The Mount's supportive community ensures help is always available
